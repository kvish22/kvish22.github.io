About

Biotechnology researcher with more than 7 years of experience in plant-microbe interactions, microbial biotechnology, plant physiology under stress, metagenomics, and molecular biology. My work sits at the intersection of laboratory experimentation and computational analysis — using beneficial microorganisms, bioinoculant development, and microbial community profiling to understand how microbes shape plant performance and support sustainable agriculture.

My long-term goal is to contribute to an interdisciplinary, research-intensive academic environment by integrating plant-microbial interactions, bioinformatics, next-generation sequencing, and multi-omics approaches — translating basic research into applied biotechnology, and building collaborative programs focused on microbiome-driven sustainable solutions and environmental resilience.

Research Interests

  • Plant stress resilience and sustainable agriculture
  • Microbial ecology and host-microbe interactions
  • Microbial responses to environmental change (nutrient dynamics, abiotic stress)
  • Computational microbial ecology and ecological modelling
  • Multi-omics integration and network inference
  • Data-driven and AI-assisted approaches in microbial ecology
